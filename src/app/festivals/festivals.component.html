Festivals
Tihar, the festival of lights & colors

The five-day festival of lights, known as Tihar honors Yama, the God of Death, meanwhile the worship of Laxmi, the Goddess of Wealth dominates the festivities.

Celebrated on - 23rd to 27th Oct

Indra Jatra

This is the exciting festival of chariot processions, masked dances, religious enactments, lights, color & Living Goddess Kumari as the center of celebrations.

Celebrated on - 9th Sept

Dashain

A two-week long festival, honoring the Universal Mother Goddess Durga with prayers, offerings, and pujas. It's a time for family reunions, gift exchanges, and celebrates the harvest season.

Celebrated on - 26th to 8th Oct

Gai Jatra

Gai Jatra is a joyful festival in Kathmandu Valley to honor loved ones who passed away. It involves dancing, singing, and children dressed as cows parading on the streets. It usually takes place in July or August.

Celebrated on - Jul / Aug

Yomari punhi

Yomari Punhi is an auspicious day for the Newari community. On this day Kathmandu Valley residents worship Annapurna, the goddess of crops and food, and pray for a good rice harvest.
